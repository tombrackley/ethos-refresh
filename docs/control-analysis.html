Control — three-layer hierarchy for the leader's view

Home is everyone's Personal Dashboard — settled. Control is the new admin / leader surface. The first draft of this brief inventoried every possible tile onto one flat dashboard. That isn't a hierarchy — it's a wall. This v2 keeps the breadth but arranges Control as three stacked layers, each answering one question the leader actually has.

Position

Two surfaces, not one. Don't conflate them.

Home
Personal Dashboard

"What do I need to do today?"

Universal landing for every authenticated user. Greeting, today's calendar, to-do, recent activity, quick actions. Already shipped.

Control
Org performance lens

"How is the organisation actually doing?"

Admin / leader only. Signal aggregation across all spaces. Role-gated and access-logged.

One sentence

Home is for my day. Control is for our org. Same person, two reasons to look at Ethos.

Who sees Control

Access is granted by Org Admin to control-authority roles. Non-authority users don't see the Control sidebar entry, and URL navigation returns "Not authorised".

  • Default authority roles: CEO, COO, GC, CRO, Practice Manager, Team Leader.
  • Board roles: Board Chair / Committee Chair — configurable per board.
  • Delegated: an authority user can grant read-only Control access to a colleague for a limited time (e.g. board-pack prep) — Phase 2.
  • AI agents: no Control authority. The Org Health Score is a read-only output, not a decision.

The hierarchy

Three layers, stacked on one page. Each layer has a single job. A leader can stop at Layer 1 most days; descends only when the headline tells them something needs attention.

Why a hierarchy beats a flat grid

A flat dashboard with 20 tiles forces the leader to scan all of them on every visit because nothing tells them which to look at first. A hierarchy says start here, descend only if needed. The information is the same; the cognitive load is much lower.

Layer 1

The check-in — always visible, above the fold

Answers "Is anything on fire? What shifted?" — the 5-second read.

Three things only. Identical for every leader. If everything here is green and nothing's pending, the leader closes the tab — that's a win.

Org Health Score

Composite gauge with trend. Click opens a breakdown drawer — components, contributions, "what changed since last view".

What changed since last view

Timeline of org-level events — regulatory updates, control failures, new policies, board decisions.

Needs my attention

Ranked list of items where I'm the named approver or owner. Cross-space, sorted by impact, not by source.

Layer 2

Score component zones — the spine

Answers "Where is the issue?" — diagnostic, score-component-keyed.

The key idea: the five Org Health Score components ARE the information architecture for the body of the page. Not arbitrary tile groups. Score drops 4 points? The drawer says why, and the component zone underneath has the detail. Each zone has the same shape: a summary row, 2–3 supporting tiles, and a "Jump to space" link.

Compliance Obligations coverage, overdue items, attestations, regulatory horizon

Tiles: Obligations posture · Overdue items · Attestation rate · Regulatory horizon · industry sub-tiles (FAR, CPS 510, ASX continuous disclosure, ACNC standards, TGA reporting…)

Risk Heatmap, top movers, incidents, coverage gaps

Tiles: Risk heatmap · Top movers since last view · Open incidents & breaches · Coverage gaps

Audit readiness Findings, evidence, cadence

Tiles: Open findings · Evidence completeness · Time-to-close · Audit cadence

Control effectiveness Tests, exceptions, ownership, conflicts

Tiles: Tests due · Exceptions · Controls without owners · Conflicts outstanding (an unresolved conflict is a control failure)

Integration health Uptime, sync, feed quality, admin signals

Tiles: Source-system uptime · Last successful sync · Failed feeds · Org Admin signals (security alerts, billing exceptions)

Industry packs render as content swaps inside the relevant component zone — FAR inside Compliance, AML/CTF inside Compliance + Risk, modern slavery reporting inside Compliance. There is no separate "Industry" section at this layer; that's what creates the "wall of industry tiles" problem.

Layer 3

The operating view — parallel section, role-shaped

Answers "How are we operating?" — cross-business operating metrics that aren't part of the AI-aggregated score.

These tiles are real, useful, and live in the spaces they belong to. Control surfaces them in one place because the leader needs the rollup. They sit in a clearly-labelled section beneath Layer 2 so they don't dilute the score's diagnostic spine.

Team workload & capacity

Per-person utilisation, bench depth, hot-spots.

Action throughput by space

Items opened vs closed, last 30 days. Trend per space.

Matter health

Pipeline, at-risk, profitability. Lawfirm-prominent.

Contract review queue

Backlog, SLA, complexity. In-house legal-prominent.

Board / committee cadence

Upcoming meetings, papers due, decisions pending.

CPD risk indicator

Directors / staff at risk of missing requirements. Govpro-prominent.

How role shapes each layer

Persona shaping is now a much smaller surface area. Layer 1 is universal. Layer 2 changes only what's expanded by default and which industry sub-tiles render. Layer 3 is where persona really pulls.

Layer 2 — which component is expanded on first load

All five zones are always present (the score has five components for every leader). Persona controls which open by default; the others are one click away. This stops the leader having to scroll on first visit to find what they care about.

How industry shapes Control

Industry pack(s) are configured by Org Admin at onboarding. They drive content swaps inside Layer 2 component zones — not new tiles, not a separate layer. A widget like "Regulatory horizon" stays in its slot inside Compliance; its body swaps from generic to APRA-specific to ASX-specific. Orgs with multiple packs (e.g. a listed pharma) render both pack sub-tiles inside the relevant zones; each is collapsible.

The two axes compose without bloating

A CRO at a listed pharma still gets the inhousegrc Layer 2 defaults (Risk + Audit + Controls expanded) and the Listed + Health content swaps (continuous disclosure inside Compliance; clinical incidents inside Risk). The page shape is the same as every other leader's. Only the contents differ.

Handoffs into and out of Control

Control is a hub, not an island. Two handoffs anchor it to the rest of the platform.

  • In — "Analyse in Control" from Insights. Each Insights item carries an "Analyse in Control" CTA. Clicking it opens Control with the relevant Layer 2 component expanded and the Insights context pre-loaded — e.g. an FAR update lands on Compliance, FAR sub-tile highlighted, policy diff in the side drawer. v1 = land on tile with context. Phase 2 = full Impact Modeller / scenario simulator.
  • Out — "Jump to space" on every component zone. Each Layer 2 zone has a link that takes the leader from signal to the underlying records — Comply, Govern, Work, Vault — with the same filter applied.

Inline quick actions in the page header keep the path back to action short: Start meeting · Create task · Ask AI · Jump to space.

Cross-cutting requirements

  • Entitlement-aware tiles. Tiles that aggregate from Board Confidential records are suppressed for non-board users (or shown with an "access scoped" footer). The Org Health Score is computed using only the requesting user's entitled data.
  • Audit log every access. Control View opens are logged for ISO 42001 evidence — who, when, which score state was visible.
  • Score reconciliation. The Score Verifier (2nd line agent) checks components reconcile to source data. A reconciliation failure is a measured AIMS incident, not a UI bug.
  • Print & export. A leader needs to drop the score view into a board pack — single-page print stylesheet and PDF export are table stakes.
  • Sticky Layer 1. The check-in stays visible as the leader scrolls into Layer 2 / 3. They never lose the headline.

Open questions to lock in before build

  1. Score scope under entitlement

    For a leader who can't see Board Confidential matters, does the Org Health Score reflect their entitled view only, or the full org with classified contributions abstracted? Both are defensible; the answer shapes the score's contract and what the breakdown drawer says.

  2. Multi-board roll-up for govpro

    For a director sitting on multiple boards, does Control roll up across all boards by default or scope to a selected board? Cross-board roll-up is more useful but harder to make trustworthy.

  3. Precedence when multiple industry packs collide

    When two packs both define a sub-tile (e.g. AML/CTF in Profserv and Financial services non-APRA), which one wins inside Compliance? Probably most-specific by jurisdiction, but it needs an explicit rule.

  4. Personalisation vs consistency

    Can a leader hide / re-order Layer 3 tiles within their persona, or is the order fixed for consistency across leaders of the same persona? Phase 2 question; impacts whether the tile registry needs a per-user store.

  5. "What changed since last view" baseline

    Per-leader last-viewed timestamp, or a fixed 24h diff? Per-leader is more useful but adds storage and a "you weren't here for two weeks" edge case. 24h is enough for v1.

  6. Industry tile authoring & ownership

    Which content team writes the regulatory references (FAR templates, CPS 230 checkpoints, TGA reporting fields)? Sourcing this is the long pole on industry-pack quality at GA.

  7. Layer 2 default expansion behaviour

    Should the persona defaults be hard-coded for v1, or should a leader's last-expanded set persist? Persisting is friendlier but means two leaders of the same persona see different first views — which can be confusing if they share a call.
